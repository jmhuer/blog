Formal Defenition¶

Semidefinite Programming (SDP) has the form

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{ll} \operatorname{minimize} & C \bullet X \\ \text { s.t. } & A_{i} \bullet X=b_{i} \quad i=1, \ldots, m \\ & X \succeq 0 \end{array}\end{split}\]
\[C \bullet X:=\sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} C_{i j} X_{i j}\]

Linear Programming and SDP¶

A linear program LP is a special instance of an SDP. This means the LP can be written as an SDP

\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} &\operatorname{minimize} \quad C \bullet X\\ &\begin{array}{ll} \text { s.t. } & A_{i} \bullet X=b_{i}\quad i=1, \ldots, m \\ & X_{i j}=0 \quad i=1, \ldots, n \quad j=i+1, \ldots, n \\ & X \succeq 0 \end{array} \end{aligned}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}C=\left(\begin{array}{cccc} c_{1} & 0 & \ldots & 0 \\ 0 & c_{2} & \ldots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \ldots & c_{n} \end{array}\right) \quad X=\left(\begin{array}{cccc} x_{1} & 0 & \ldots & 0 \\ 0 & x_{2} & \ldots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \ldots & x_{n} \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} A_{i}=\left(\begin{array}{cccc} a_{i 1} & 0 & \ldots & 0 \\ 0 & a_{i 2} & \ldots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \ldots & a_{i n} \end{array}\right) \quad i=1, \ldots, m \end{aligned}\end{split}\]
